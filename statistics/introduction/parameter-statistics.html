Parameter and Statistic

In statistics, it is important to distinguish between parameters and statistics. A parameter is a numerical value that describes a characteristic of an entire population. For example, the average height of all students in a school or the overall standard deviation of income in a country are parameters. These values are often represented using Greek letters, such as Œº (mu) for the population mean and œÉ (sigma) for the population standard deviation.
